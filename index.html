About Me

I am currently a senior physics major at Carthage College. Since my first year as an undergraduate I have been involved in the development of a technology that measures the amount of fuel in a propellant tank via modal analysis. I was selected to intern with the Advanced Engineering Development Branch at NASA Kennedy Space Center during Summer 2020 to design a payload to implement this fuel gauging technology for testing on the International Space Station.

As my undergraduate career comes to an end, I am applying to graduate schools to pursue my PhD in Aerospace Engineering.

Internship Abstract

Modal Propellant Gauging on the International Space Station (MPG-ISS)

I was selected for an internship at NASA Kennedy Space Center Advanced Engineering Development Branch during summer 2020 to design a payload that will take flight on a SpaceX Dragon Capsule to stay onboard the International Space Station for 30-60 days. During my 10 week internship, I was able to work with a team of engineers to design a payload that implements Modal Propellant Gauging (MPG). MPG is a lightweight, cost-efficient, high resolution fuel gauging system for low-gravity environments. I designed and modeled the experiment using Autodesk Inventor and gained valuable skills in the software.

  Photo courtesy of Blue Origin

Mission Team Lead, Data Acquisition and Analyst Lead, Mechanical Lead

Modal Propellant Gauging - Blue Origin Payload (MPG-BOP)

I have spent the majority of my undergraduate career helping develop MPG and preparing this payload for flight on two suborbital missions on Blue Origin's New Shepard Vehicle in January and December of 2019. I joined MPG-BOP during the second semester of my freshman year and started work on the experiment as a mechanical team member. I thoroughly enjoyed working on the mechanical design such as updating CAD files in SolidWorks and making changes to the physical system to improve the payload.

Throughout the next year, I worked my way to becoming Mission Team Lead of the MPG-BOP experiment.

Data Acquisition and Analyst Lead, Mechanical Team Member

Modal Propellant Gauging - Propellant Refueling and on Orbit Transfer Operations (MPG-PROTO)

Designed, built, and tested a payload to further improve a fuel gauging method called Modal Propellant Gauging (MPG). Used 3D modeling software (Autodesk Inventor) to assist in creating models of the payload. Sourced majority of parts for the construction of the payload and lead the mechanical build. Currently working with other team members to prepare the payload for flight on Blue Origin's New Shepard Vehicle in the coming year. MPG-PROTO was successfully tested on the Zero G flights in November 2020.

Mission Team Lead, Mechanical Lead
